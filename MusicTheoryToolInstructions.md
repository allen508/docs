[Music theory tool](https://rococo-trifle-2ec14a.netlify.app/song5)

## Finding notes on a scale

1. Choose a "Scale"
2. Choose a "Mode"
3. Choose a "Root" note 

The notes on the scale are aligned with the Mode indicator are in Grey colour.

### Your turn
1. Select "Major", "Aeolian" (AKA minor) and "C#"

The notes the key of C# minor are: 
- C#, D#, E, F#, G#, A, B

Try "Major", "Dorian", "F".


## Finding Chord notes

1. Choose a "Scale"
2. Choose a "Mode"
3. Choose a "Root" note 
4. Choose a "Triad" 

The chord notes are highlighted in a Blue colour on the interval circle.

### Your turn
1. Select "Major", "Aeolian" (AKA minor) and "C#", "Triad"

The triad chord in the key of C# minor are: 
- C#, E, G#

Add an A, and you have an extented 9th chord

Try "Major", "Locrian", "F#".

## Finding intervals within a key

Many times, you'll use a chord progression, when soloing over the chord progression its helpful to know the interval from the progression.

For instance, Select "Major", "Ionian" (AKA Major), and "C". Play the triad notes on your instrument. (C, E, G).

Now click the "5th" interval note on the circle (G). The interval wheel spins an locks to G. You can now find the Flat 7th of G (F) whilst remaining in Key of C Ionian.
